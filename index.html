Welcome

This seminar, created by English and Drama Teacher Panagiotis Domvros, offers a comprehensive professional development program for educators. The material is structured into interactive modules covering core areas of teaching, from classroom management to digital integration.

Core Pedagogical Skills

The seminar begins with lesson organization and planning, framing it as a "game plan" that requires flexibility and creativity. It emphasizes fostering student motivation through varied lesson design and includes icebreakers and reflective activities like the "Two-Word Check-In" and "The Quiet Impact" to build a positive classroom environment.

Classroom and Stakeholder Management

A significant portion is dedicated to providing practical, script-based strategies for managing challenging interactions.

  • "Difficult" Students: This module focuses on proactive and empathetic approaches. It includes activities for co-creating classroom norms ("Adult Script"), understanding student behavior ("Empathy Mapping"), and de-escalating conflict using "Water or Gasoline" role-play scenarios. The goal is to de-escalate first and problem-solve second.
  • "Difficult" Parents: This section equips teachers with communication frameworks and templates for phone calls, emails, and meetings. It provides specific lines in both English and Greek to set boundaries, manage different parent personas (e.g., angry, anxious), and ensure communication is productive and GDPR-compliant.

Innovative Teaching Techniques

The seminar strongly advocates for using theatrical techniques to enhance learning in mixed-ability classrooms. It provides a rich bank of drama activities suitable for all age groups, such as "Conscience Alley," "Hot Seat," and "Wink Murder," to dissolve barriers and turn communication into a safe, shared experiment.

Digital Integration

Finally, the program addresses modern teaching needs by covering digital tools and hacks. It aims to help teachers combine various applications to optimize preparation and teaching, presenting links to resources that can facilitate their work.
