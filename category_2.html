Framing Script
“Behind every behaviour is a reason—even if we can’t see it. Our job is to de‑escalate first, then problem‑solve. If I change me, I change the climate.”
Activity B1 — Co‑Creating the Adult Script
Objective
Set respectful norms from day one.
Steps
Pairs draft a 1–2 sentence opener for the “Treat you like adults” talk → Share a few → Refine for clarity and respect. Debrief: Make expectations explicit; the adult move is to return to focus quickly and respectfully.

Model Scripts

Younger teens (11–13): “In this class, I will always treat you with respect — like young adults. Sometimes we all lose focus or get distracted. That’s normal. The grown‑up move is to come back quickly and kindly so we can keep learning together.”

Older teens (15–18): “In this class, I will treat you as adults: with trust, respect, and high expectations. Adults sometimes slip, but the mature choice is to reset quickly and move forward. If we all do that, we’ll have the freedom to learn in a way that really prepares us for what comes next.”

            Infographic & Article — Roots of Teacher Burnout

            Summary

            This piece explains the current wave of teacher burnout as a downstream effect of a wider pupil mental-health crisis. What looks like “misbehaviour” is often dysregulation—a nervous system under stress trying to cope. Escalation and punishment can intensify the cycle; connection helps to break it.

            • From control to curiosity: Ask what need the behaviour is signalling (safety, predictability, belonging, competence).
            • Regulate → Relate → Reason: Help pupils calm first, connect second, then return to learning and problem-solving.
            • Classroom moves: predictable routines, calm tone, brief choices, short breaks, and quick wins that restore success.
            • Staff wellbeing: when teachers shift from a punishment mindset to empathy and connection, stress drops and learning improves.

            Activity B3 — Water or Gasoline? Role‑Play
            Objective
            Practise de‑escalation by contrasting heat‑adding vs heat‑reducing lines.
            Run
            A = student (“This is stupid.”), B = teacher → Round 1: Gasoline → Reset → Round 2: Water → Switch roles. Debrief: Emotions shift fast—aim to defuse.

            Example Bank (Greece‑ready, phone ban compliant)

            1) Refusal / Task Avoidance
            • Student: “This is stupid.”
            • Gasoline: “If you don’t like it, leave.”
            • Water: “Sounds frustrating. What’s one small change to help you start—time, partner, or question number?”
            2) Flat Refusal
            • Student: “I’m not doing it.”
            • Gasoline: “Yes you are. Now.”
            • Water: “Help me see the block—instructions, time, or something else? Start with Q1 together or take 2 minutes and try?”
            3) Head Down, Silent
            • Student: (head on desk)
            • Gasoline: “Up. Now.”
            • Water: (quietly) “You look worn out. First one together, or a 2‑minute pause then try?”
            4) Phone Visible (ban in force)
            • Student: (phone on desk) “I’m just checking.”
            • Gasoline: “Give me the phone.”
            • Water: “Phones must be off and away by law and school policy. Put it in your bag now. If it reappears, we follow the hand‑in procedure.”
            5) “It’s an Emergency” (still banned)
            • Student: “It’s important.”
            • Gasoline: “Nothing is more important than my class.”
            • Water: “I hear you. Law and school rules require phones off and away. Put it in your bag now—talk to me after class so we can involve the office if needed.”
            6) Smartwatch Notifications
            • Student: (tapping watch) “It’s just my watch.”
            • Gasoline: “Take it off now.”
            • Water: “Devices must be silent and out of sight. Slide the sleeve over it or place it in your bag. Thanks.”
            7) Filming/Recording (not allowed)
            • Student: (recording)
            • Gasoline: “Are you kidding me? Delete that now.”
            • Water: “Recording isn’t allowed. Stop, put the device away. We’ll log this with the office after the lesson.”
            8) Side‑Talk
            • Student: (whispering/laughing)
            • Gasoline: “Stop talking or get out.”
            • Water: (nearby, low voice) “Save it for 2 minutes—then you’re first to share. Deal?”
            9) “We’re Talking About the Work!”
            • Student: “We’re discussing the task!”
            • Gasoline: “No, you’re not.”
            • Water: “Great—put one sentence on paper so I can see it. I’ll check in 60 seconds.”
            10) Back‑chat / Defiance
            • Student: “You can’t make me.”
            • Gasoline: “Watch me.”
            • Water: “You don’t want to. Two ways forward: start 60 seconds with me, or start alone and I’ll check. Which gets you done faster?”
            11) Eye‑Roll / “Whatever”
            • Student: “Whatever.”
            • Gasoline: “Don’t roll your eyes at me.”
            • Water: (ignore roll) “Start number 2. I’ll be back in 90 seconds to see your first line.”
            12) Finished Early / Boasting
            • Student: “I’m done. Too easy.”
            • Gasoline: “Then stop showing off.”
            • Water: “Nice—grab a challenge card and upgrade two sentences with stronger adjectives.”
            13) “I’m Lost”
            • Student: “I don’t get it.”
            • Gasoline: “We already covered this.”
            • Water: “Thanks for telling me. Point to the exact step—let’s model just that part.”
            14) Won’t Join Group
            • Student: “I don’t want to work with them.”
            • Gasoline: “Too bad.”
            • Water: “Pick a role that suits you: timer, scribe, or coach. Which works today?”
            15) “They Don’t Listen to Me”
            • Student: “No one listens.”
            • Gasoline: “Speak louder then.”
            • Water: “We’ll do one‑turn each: 20 seconds per person. You’re second—I’ll time it.”
            16) Late to Class
            • Student: “It’s only 10 minutes.”
            • Gasoline: “Unacceptable.”
            • Water: “Grab the starter from the side table. I’ll check once you’ve got the first line.”
            17) Missing Materials
            • Student: “I forgot my book.”
            • Gasoline: “Of course you did.”
            • Water: “Take a spare from the bin. Add your name on a sticky so it stays with you.”
            18) Inappropriate Joke
            • Student: (joke during instructions)
            • Gasoline: “Grow up.”
            • Water: (half‑smile) “Park it. You get 20 seconds at the end to share the funny version.”
            19) Attempted Phone Lookup (ban)
            • Student: (sneaks phone to look up answers)
            • Gasoline: “You’re cheating!”
            • Water: “Phones are banned—away now. I want your thinking on Q3—talk me through your approach.”
            20) Presentation Freeze / Anxiety
            • Student: “I can’t do this.”
            • Gasoline: “Yes you can. Go.”
            • Water: “Okay—start the first two lines seated, then stand for the last one. Nod when ready, I’ll time you.”
            Activity B4 — Flip the Script
            Objective
            Turn directives into questions to invite collaboration and preserve face.

            Convert together

            • “Put your phone away.” → “Where should your phone be so you can focus?”
            • “Start the assignment.” → “Which question will you start with?”
            • “Stop talking.” → “Where would you like to sit to get this done?”
            Activity B5 — The 5 Metacognition Questions
            Objective
            Move from impulse to reflection.

            Core 5

            • Why did you think this was okay?
            • Who in your life thinks this is okay?
            • Where did you learn this?
            • How can you help fix this now?
            • What will you do next time?

            Extended Prompt Bank

            • Are you ready to think, or do you need 1 minute?
            • What happened just before this?
            • How did you feel? (angry / sad / bored / stressed / worried / tired)
            • Which part is your responsibility?
            • Tell me two other choices you could make next time.
            • If I feel ___, then I will ___.

            Copy worksheet text
            Full Prompt Bank

            A) Core 5

            • Why did you think this was okay?
            • Who in your life thinks this is okay?
            • Where did you learn this?
            • How can you help fix this now?
            • What will you do next time?

            B) Simple “Pause & Feelings”

            • Are you ready to think, or do you need 1 minute?
            • What happened just before this?
            • How did you feel? (angry / sad / bored / stressed / worried / tired)

            C) Ownership

            • Which part is your responsibility?
            • What was your choice in that moment?
            • If we watch a replay, where should you pause?

            D) Rules & Values

            • Which class rule did this break?
            • Does this match the person you want to be in class?
            • What does “respect” look like here? (one example)

            E) Impact

            • Who was affected? How?
            • What changed in the room because of this?
            • If you were them, how would you feel?

            F) Options

            • Tell me two other choices you could make next time.
            • What could you say to yourself to stay calm?
            • Which helps you more: move seat / ask for help / take 1 minute / start with an easy step?

            G) Repair (today)

            • What is one repair you can do today? (apology / help / redo work)
            • How will we know the repair worked?
            • Please write one sentence you will say or do.

            H) Future Plan (If–Then)

            • If I feel ____ , then I will ____ .
            • Next time I notice ____ , my first step is ____ .
            • What do you want me (the teacher) to do to help you keep this plan?

            I) Quick close

            • Say your plan back to me.
            • When should we check—end of lesson or tomorrow?
            • Thank you. Let’s join the lesson again.
            Activity B6 — The 30‑Second Rule Sort
            Objective
            Build a culture of kindness.
            Rule
            “If they can’t change it in 30 seconds, don’t mention it.”

            Sort cards (examples)

            Okay to Say
            • “Thank you for letting me speak.”
            • “Can I help you with that step?”
            • “I liked your example about travel.”
            Not Okay
            • “Your face is full of spots.”
            • “Your accent is weird.”
            • “Your shoes are ugly.”
            Quick Tools
            • Foam basketball reset: bounce 3 times; toss to attentive students.
            • Break Card: class‑wide 2‑minute reset; timer on board; all work paused; return ready.
            • Pause Moment: Stop → Reset → Address → Begin again.
            • 7 Words: “I love having you in my class.”
