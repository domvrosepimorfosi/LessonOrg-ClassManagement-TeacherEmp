Opening Script
“Many of the greatest teachers won’t win awards or fame. Their quiet, daily work changes lives. There’s no single ‘best’ teaching style; what matters is that our students feel valued and accepted in our rooms.”
Activity A1 — Two-Word Check-In (3′)
  • “I’ll start. I’m [Name]; my two words about teaching today are ‘energised’ and ‘curious.’”
  • “Your turn: name + two words about how you feel in your work right now.”
Activity A2 — The Quiet Impact (2′)
  • “Close your eyes for one minute. Recall a teacher who had a quiet, profound impact on you—focus on how they made you feel.”
  • “Open your eyes. That feeling is why we’re here.”
Activity A3 — One Word, One Story (5′)
  • “Write one word capturing a recent classroom victory—big or small.”
Activity A4 — The Student I Have (5′)
  • “Think of one current student (no names). Identify one strength and one challenge.”
  • “In pairs: 3 minutes to propose a strategy that leverages the strength.”
Module 2 — Lesson Organisation & Planning (30′)

Framing Script

  • “Treat your lesson plan as a game plan—clear outcomes, flexible execution.”
  • “Think composer–conductor: heavy creative prep in advance; in the room, you conduct and adapt.”
  • “Real learning is messy: expect tension, confusion, productive discomfort.”
Activity A5 — Curveball Scenario (7′) SPANNER IN THE WORKS
  • “Your objective: students practise descriptive adjectives. Here’s your curveball.”
  • “You have 3 minutes to adjust your plan. Go.”
  • Share 1–2 pivots per group.
Activity A6 — The 5-Minute Takeover (8′) Student Handout 5-Minute Takeover Activities
  • “Design a 5-minute activity on prepositions of place that a student can lead.”
  • Share 2–3 ideas.
  • Variations: “Walk-and-write,” quick game, peer-instruction mini-demo. (Walk-and-Write Variation (5-Minute Takeover))
  • Walk-and-Write Challenge (Student led)
  • Troubleshooting: If groups stall, suggest a simple gallery walk or ‘teach-back’.
