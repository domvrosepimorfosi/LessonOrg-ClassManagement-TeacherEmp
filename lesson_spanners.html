Sample EFL Lesson + Spanners (B1)
Overview

Sample EFL Lesson Plan (B1 level, 30′)

Topic: Descriptive Adjectives for Places and Objects

Objective: Students will be able to produce descriptive sentences with 2–3 adjectives, using digital collaboration tools.

1) Warm‑up (5′)

Tool: Padlet / “Softboard” (digital sticky notes).

  • Task: Prompt = “Write one adjective to describe your weekend.”
  • Students post 1 word each; teacher clusters them live (e.g., feelings, size, colours).
  • Quick oral recap: “We already have lots of adjectives. Today we’ll use them to make our descriptions more vivid.”
2) Presentation (5′)

Tool: Google Slides / Canva (projected).

  • Images shown: e.g., a busy street, a calm beach, an old castle.
  • Teacher elicits adjectives via Padlet live‑posts.
  • Teacher adds 2–3 higher‑level adjectives into the Padlet board for enrichment.
3) Guided Practice (10′)

Tool: Padlet columns (one column per picture).

  • Task: Students work in pairs. Each pair posts 2 descriptive sentences in the correct column.
  • Example: “The castle is ancient and mysterious.”
  • Teacher role: Likes and stars strong examples, highlights errors, models corrections.
  • Check for understanding: “Everyone add a comment on one peer’s post suggesting a stronger adjective.”
4) Independent Practice (7′)

Tool: Quizlet Live / Blooket (adjective sets prepared).

  • Students play a quick round, matching adjectives to definitions or intensities (big → enormous; tired → exhausted).
  • Extension option: Fast finishers expand a sentence on Padlet using one of the Quizlet words.
5) Reflection & Closing (3′)

Tool: Padlet final column titled “Exit Ticket.”

  • Students post one new adjective they will use in their next piece of writing.
  • Teacher summarises: “You’ve shown you can use adjectives not just to label, but to bring your descriptions alive.”

Tech Issues

Wi‑Fi goes down — no online tools

Pivot (Plan B)

  • Switch to low‑tech fallback: sticky notes on the wall instead of Padlet.
  • Draw quick sketches (castle, street, beach) on board or show printed pictures.
  • Adjective brainstorm → students cluster words by category on the wall.

What to say

“Looks like the Wi‑Fi is down. No problem—we’re going analogue. Grab 3 sticky notes. Write one adjective per note. Put them on the wall under the correct picture. Let’s see how many we can collect in 2 minutes.”
Projector fails — can’t show Google Slides/Canva

Pivot (Plan B)

  • Print 3–4 images beforehand as a backup.
  • Hand them to groups; each group generates adjectives on paper.
  • If no prints, describe the scene orally: “Imagine a castle on a cliff at sunset. Give me three adjectives.”

What to say

“We don’t have visuals, so we’ll use words to paint pictures. Imagine: an old castle on a cliff at sunset. Call out adjectives that bring it alive.”
Padlet freezes — posts don’t appear

Pivot (Plan B)

  • Switch to a shared Google Doc (if Wi‑Fi works).
  • Or each pair writes sentences on paper, then one representative posts them aloud.
  • Teacher writes sentences on the board in columns.

What to say

“Padlet isn’t cooperating. Let’s move to paper and board. Write 2 sentences with adjectives. I’ll collect them and group them by picture.”
Quizlet Live server crash — can’t start the game

Pivot (Plan B)

  • Teacher‑led quick‑fire synonyms: “Say a stronger word than big.”
  • Pairs do “synonym ladder”: tired → sleepy → exhausted → shattered.
  • If possible, use a pre‑printed matching exercise.

What to say

“Quizlet’s down. Let’s do our own live game—synonym ladder. Start with happy; what’s stronger? Joyful? Ecstatic? Keep climbing.”

Classroom / Logistics Issues

Half the students finish the Padlet task in 3′ while others are stuck

Pivot (Plan B)

  • Fast finishers: write one upgraded sentence using a precise adjective (“big” → “enormous”).
  • Or comment on 2 peers’ posts with suggestions.

What to say

“If you’re done, add one ‘upgrade adjective’ to your sentence. Or leave a comment on 2 peers’ posts to suggest stronger words.”
Two students post silly/inappropriate adjectives on Padlet

Pivot (Plan B)

  • Pause moment: “Stop. Reset. Begin again.”
  • Quietly remind: “Padlet is a shared space; posts must be usable in class. Let’s re‑enter.”
  • If repeated, assign those students to scribe others’ suggestions on paper.

What to say

“This space is public and collaborative. Our goal is real adjectives we can reuse. Let’s reset and try again.”
A group refuses to engage with Quizlet Live

Pivot (Plan B)

  • Give them a mini challenge: “You’ve got 3 minutes to find 5 synonyms for nice without the game.”
  • Let them be “judges”: they check other teams’ adjectives for accuracy.

What to say

“If Quizlet’s not your thing, you’re now the judges. Check that each team uses their adjectives correctly in sentences.”
Noise levels rise — students distracted

Pivot (Plan B)

  • Use attention reset: bounce a foam ball or clap a rhythm; students repeat.
  • Silent 30‑second reset: “We pause. We reset posture. We begin again.”

What to say

“Pause moment. Everyone take 10 seconds to reset. Sit tall, look at me. We begin again.”
Devices run out of battery / 3 students have no phones

Pivot (Plan B)

  • Device‑less students join a peer’s group as Scribe/Reader.
  • Or teacher appoints them as Board Writers, transferring group answers onto the board.

What to say

“No device? Perfect—you’re the scribe. Your job is to collect ideas and post them for your group.”
Teacher’s own laptop dies mid‑lesson

Pivot (Plan B)

  • Use student devices to project (if possible).
  • If not, switch to board‑led activity: teacher calls out objects/places; students supply adjectives orally or on paper.

What to say

“My laptop’s taking a break. Let’s see how many adjectives we can generate together on the board—faster than tech!”

Timing / Flow Issues

An unexpected announcement cuts 7 minutes

Pivot (Plan B)

  • Drop Guided Practice share‑out OR skip Quizlet round.
  • Go straight to Reflection with Padlet Exit Ticket.

What to say

“We’ve lost 7 minutes—so we’ll cut Quizlet and go straight to the Exit Ticket.”
Students take much longer than expected to log into Padlet

Pivot (Plan B)

  • Switch to oral brainstorm → teacher writes on board.
  • Let students log in quietly while others share aloud.

What to say

“While you log in, shout out one adjective. I’ll collect them on the board. If you can’t connect, write them on paper.”
